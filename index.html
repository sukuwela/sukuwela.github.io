Speaks Web Application

Overview:

  • Summary: Children with autism or other learning disabilities have difficulty understanding social queues and social situations, as well as communication. In order to improve such, our team is developing an application that teaches children about different social positions such as firefighter or mailman, so that they can better understand these scenarios as well as communicate them to others.
  • Objective: To help children with learning disabilities improve their social and communication skills through an easy and accessible web application.
  • Approach:The children are provided with various social scenarios to choose from such as firefighter, policeman, mailman and more. After choosing a scenario, the child will answer questions that ask to match the correct tools the firefighter, policeman, etc. will need in order to perform their job. The child will be guided with helpful hints if they are incorrect to help them along.
  • Project Duration: January 2018 - March 2018
  • My Role: I performed qualitative interviews with teachers and aids working with children with autism remotely to ensure autism-friendly design. I also tested this application along with my team members, with children with autism to ensure that our design was actually accessibile and usable, as well as any changes that need to be made going forward with the application.
  • Links: User Interviews, Speaks High-Fidelity Prototype
  • Final Product: Here (Application still in development)
